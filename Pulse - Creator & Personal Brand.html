№ 01 Identity

The single biggest ICP segment Lessgo will serve, and the one that current SaaS-templated landing pages get most wrong.

Pulse is for the one human, with their hands free. The newsletter writer with 18,000 subscribers. The founder-CEO who's also a public thinker. The executive coach who takes seven clients a quarter. The podcaster, the author, the freelance consultant.

The job isn't to make their site look like a company. It's to make it look like them, on a good day.

What that costs us: every default that assumes a "team," "products," "logos." What it earns us: a system the buyer's audience instantly trusts because it doesn't pretend to be more than what it is.

Five commitments

  • Big portrait, big name, big claim. Hero is a face, a name, and a one-line who-I-am. Not "I help X do Y" template phrasing.
  • "Where I publish," not "as seen in." The press strip cites venues where they write, speak, show up — not borrowed authority.
  • A /now feeling. Three things they're working on this month, dated. Honest, not performative.
  • Offerings as cards with honest fit. Free / one-time / 1:1. Each says who it's for and who it's not.
  • Archive of writing as primary content. Words are the asset. The archive section treats them that way.

№ 02 Type

Fraunces (variable) for display — italic-by-default, soft optical sizing. Inter for body. Marker highlights for emphasis.

Variants

  • Voice — Fraunces italic display, soft optical setting. Reads literary.
  • Operator — Fraunces upright, weight 600, hard optical setting. Reads decisive.
  • Coach — Fraunces italic, larger optical size. Reads warmer, more spacious.

№ 04 Decoration

Six permitted motifs. All borrowed from personal-essay typography. All used sparingly.

  • Pulse dot. Animated dot for "live now," "publishing weekly," nav-brand finial. Never decorative on cards.
  • Highlighter mark. A single phrase per heading. Yellow only, no other colors.
  • Italic-as-emphasis. Variable Fraunces SOFT axis flips between italic-soft (literary) and roman-hard (decisive) at the variant level.
  • Press strip italics. Venue names set in display italic, never logos. The buyer's audience knows what NYT looks like.
  • Status chips. Live · soon · paused. Border-only, single dot, no fill colors.
  • Numbered archive. № notation for posts. Italic mile-marker numerals (i, ii, iii) reserved for letters/clients elsewhere.

What's banned: stock-photo "Chris in front of laptop" hero shots, "AI-powered creator" anything, three-column "Read/Listen/Watch" trios with logo lockups, hidden newsletter signups, "as seen in" with stale logo bars, two-accent palettes (Riot territory), gradient backgrounds.

Tokens, copy & paste.

The full token surface for the renderer. The user-configurable axes are [data-palette] (9 options) and [data-variant] (Voice · Operator · Coach). Display family is Fraunces variable across all variants; the SOFT and italic axes flip per variant.

:root tokens

/* Surface */
--bg:           oklch(0.99 0.005 80);       /* paper */
--bg-1:         oklch(0.965 0.008 80);
--ink:          oklch(0.16 0.01 250);       /* near-black */
--ink-2:        oklch(0.32 0.01 250);       /* body */
--rule:         oklch(0.85 0.008 250);      /* hairline */

/* Accent — one color, <= 4% of viewport */
--accent:       oklch(0.55 0.22 255);       /* electric blue (default) */
--accent-deep:  oklch(0.42 0.24 258);
--highlight:    oklch(0.93 0.18 95);        /* marker yellow — fixed */

/* Type */
--font-display: "Fraunces", "Iowan Old Style", serif;
--font-body:    "Inter", system-ui, sans-serif;
--font-mono:    "JetBrains Mono", monospace;

/* Radius — soft, not hard */
--r-sm: 6px;  --r-md: 10px;
--r-lg: 16px; --r-pill: 999px;
