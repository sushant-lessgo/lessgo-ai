Identity

Who it's for, what it feels like.

Naayom targets hardware companies selling monitoring & control systems to operators who run a physical process — mushroom farms, greenhouses, cold storage, hydroponics, aquaculture. The buyer is a grower or facility manager, not a CTO. The page must feel credible and measured: it shows real sensor numbers, names equipment by model, talks yield and energy, and routes to a human on WhatsApp rather than a checkout. Trust comes from specificity, not polish.

Read it like an instrument panel, not a brochure. Every number is real, every photo is a working install, every claim has a unit attached.

Usage rules

  • Forest — primary brand fill: nav CTA, dark sections (Problem, Gallery, CTA, footer), button fills, headings on paper.
  • Lime — the data signal, used sparingly: primary CTA on dark, sensor sparklines, status dots, eyebrows (lime-d on light for contrast), one highlight word in the hero.
  • Teal — secondary data state only (e.g. "driving"/equipment). Never decorative.
  • Forbidden: bright lime as a body or large-area background (it's a signal, not a surface), gradients-as-decoration, more than one lime CTA per viewport.

Signature: the live-readout card

The motif that makes the product feel measurable. Reused 3×.

A small instrument panel: a status row (in-range pill + chamber + stage), a 3-up mono metric grid (CO₂ · Temp · RH, each with a unit), and a footer sparkline with a mono caption. Values tick gently on a timer to read "live" (disabled under prefers-reduced-motion). It anchors the hero, then recurs in How it works and Compatibility showing different states (sensing vs. driving equipment).

Other components

  • Status pill — mono uppercase, pulsing dot. ok (green, "optimal/in-range") and teal ("driving").
  • Buttons — fill (forest), lime (primary action), line (outline), wa (WhatsApp green), ghost-d (on dark).
  • Photo placeholder — striped .ph block with a centered mono tag naming the exact shot. Light and .on-dark variants.

Decorative layer

Instrument cues, never ornament.

  • Diagonal hairline stripes — fill every photo placeholder; read as technical hatching.
  • Corner frame — a thin lime inset rectangle on the hero unit, like a viewfinder / registration mark.
  • Sparklines — lime trend lines in readouts; the only "chart" on the page.
  • Pulsing status dot — single source of motion alongside the live-ticking values.
  • Mono index numbers — 01 / 02 / 03 on the problem list and steps.

Copy voice

Grower-to-grower. Numbers over adjectives.

Plain, concrete, operator-first. Lead with the consequence a grower feels — aborted pins, a missed 3am window, a power bill — then the fix. Attach units to claims ("+50% yield", "−31% kWh", "820 ppm"). No "revolutionize," no "unlock," no "smart" as filler. Testimonials name a farm and a city. CTAs offer a conversation, never a price.

Specificity is the whole brand. "Stage-based control of CO₂, temp and RH" beats "intelligent climate optimization" every time.

Page structure

The ordered section system this template ships with.

  • Nav — slim, sticky; Platform-login (lock) + Book-a-demo.
  • Hero — split: highlight headline + proof chip + dual CTA, with controller photo and an overlaid live-readout.
  • Trust strip — headline metrics + customer logo rail.
  • Problem — dark forest section, 3 numbered pains.
  • How it works — Sense → Control → Optimize, 3 connected steps.
  • Capabilities — 6-cell hairline grid.
  • Product lineup — 3 labeled rows (controllers · control systems · monitors), "Enquire" not pricing.
  • Results — 3 stat cards + 3 testimonials.
  • Gallery — dark masonry of field photos.
  • Compatibility — retrofit note + two readout states.
  • FAQ — 5 disclosures.
  • CTA band — demo + WhatsApp (prominent) + phone.
  • Footer — brand, contact, product/company/support columns.

Configure for a client

The knobs to turn when reusing this template. Everything else is baked.

brand
Wordmark + mark in nav/footer, page <title>, and the favicon-style .brand .mk dot.
--forest / --lime
Two hue knobs. Shift the brand & accent hues in :root; keep lightness/chroma so contrast holds.
contact
Phone & WhatsApp (currently +91 99999 99999 / wa.me/…), address, email, platform-login URL.
product lineup
Model rows & cards — line names, model codes, specs. Add/remove cards in the grid.
metrics & copy
Trust numbers, stats, testimonials, FAQ — swap to the client's real figures and quotes.
imagery
Replace every .ph with a real photo (controllers, installs, dashboard, flushes). Tags name each slot.
readouts
Metric labels/units in the readout cards — match the client's sensed variables.
sale model
No pricing by default. If the client lists prices, the product cards are the place to add them.

The problem

Yield walks out the door every time the climate drifts.

Mushrooms don't forgive a swing in CO₂ or a dry afternoon. Most farms still run on hand-written logs, wall thermostats and a person who can never leave.

01

Manual monitoring can't keep up

Walking chambers with a handheld meter catches problems hours late. By the time RH has dropped, the pins have already aborted — and nobody was there at 3am.

02

Inconsistent flushes, unpredictable crop loss

One chamber flushes heavy, the next stalls. Without stage-based control you can't reproduce your best batch — so revenue swings with the weather and crop loss eats the margin.

03

Energy burned, and you're chained to the farm

Compressors and blowers run flat-out instead of to setpoint, wasting power. And someone has to physically be on site — every night, every weekend — just to keep conditions in range.

How it works

Sense. Control. Optimize.

One closed loop per chamber. Sensors read the climate, the controller drives your equipment to setpoint, and analytics tighten the recipe over every cycle.

Common questions

Before you book a demo.

Will it work with my existing equipment?

Almost always. Naayom is built to retrofit — it wires into the compressors, blowers, foggers, valves and VFDs you already run. During the demo we'll go through your setup and confirm exactly what's needed.

How long does installation take?

Monitoring units are typically live within a day. Full stage-based control across a room usually takes about a week, depending on how many chambers and what equipment is in place. Our team handles commissioning and calibration.

What does it monitor and control?

CO₂, temperature and humidity per chamber, with stage-based setpoints for colonization, pinning and flowering. Controllers drive your equipment automatically; monitors sense and alert. Everything streams to one dashboard you can reach from anywhere.

Can I start small and scale up?

Yes. Many farms start with an NWM monitor on one room to prove it out, then add NWC controllers as they expand. Every unit talks to the same platform, so nothing gets thrown away as you grow.

What support do I get after install?

24/7 remote access plus a support team that understands mushroom farms. Threshold alerts reach you by phone and WhatsApp, and we're available to help tune recipes and troubleshoot equipment.
