🔥 PAINS (What hurts most, in their words)
1. Leads are getting wasted or mishandled

This is by far the loudest pain across dealer discussions.

Real dealer language:

“Leads are coming in but nobody follows up properly.”

“Internet leads die on the vine.”

“By the time my guy calls, the customer already bought somewhere else.”

Key insight:

Dealers believe they’re paying for leads they never truly work.

2. Speed-to-lead is inconsistent (and killing deals)

Dealers repeatedly complain that:

sales reps don’t respond fast enough

follow-ups are forgotten

response quality varies wildly by salesperson

Common phrases:

“First one to respond wins.”

“If you don’t hit them in 5 minutes, you’re done.”

“My best closers aren’t always the best responders.”

3. Sales staff turnover and inconsistency

Managers are frustrated that:

new hires don’t follow process

veterans don’t want to use the CRM

training never sticks

Dealer voices:

“Every new guy has to relearn everything.”

“Processes fall apart when one person quits.”

“I can’t scale what my top performer does.”

4. Phones go unanswered (especially after hours)

This comes up constantly.

Language you’ll see:

“Calls go to voicemail way too often.”

“After hours = missed opportunities.”

“BDC is overloaded or understaffed.”

They know:

Missed calls = missed money.

5. CRM fatigue & tool overload

Dealers complain that:

CRMs are clunky

reps don’t log activity

managers don’t trust the data

Common phrasing:

“If it’s not simple, my guys won’t use it.”

“CRM says we followed up — reality says otherwise.”

🌱 DESIRES (What they want, bluntly)
1. Every lead answered instantly — every time

This is non-negotiable.

Desired outcomes:

instant replies

consistent messaging

no dependence on human availability

Dealer language:

“I want every lead hit immediately.”

“I don’t care who answers — just answer fast.”

2. Consistent, professional sales conversations

They want:

no dumb replies

no typos

no attitude

no forgotten follow-ups

Phrases:

“Say the right thing, every time.”

“Handle objections properly.”

“Qualify without pissing people off.”

3. Less reliance on humans for repetitive work

Managers openly admit:

humans are unreliable

burnout is real

repetitive tasks get skipped

Language:

“I don’t need humans doing copy-paste follow-ups.”

“Let my closers close.”

4. More appointments showing up

Not just booked — actually showing up.

Common phrases:

“Appointments are weak.”

“They book but don’t show.”

“I want better-qualified appointments.”

5. Something that actually works with their dealership

They want:

minimal setup

integration with existing CRM/DMS

no long onboarding hell

Dealer voice:

“I don’t have time for another system my guys won’t use.”

🚧 OBJECTIONS (Why they’re skeptical)
1. “This sounds like another shiny tool”

Dealers are extremely vendor-fatigued.

Objection language:

“Every vendor promises higher close rates.”

“I’ve heard this pitch before.”

“Show me results, not demos.”

2. Fear of losing the human touch

Especially for higher-ticket buyers.

Concerns:

“Car sales is still a people business.”

“I don’t want robots turning customers off.”

They’re okay with automation as long as it doesn’t feel robotic.

3. Worry about compliance and brand risk

Dealers are cautious about:

incorrect pricing

bad promises

misrepresentation

Phrases:

“What if it says the wrong thing?”

“I can’t have AI quoting the wrong numbers.”

4. Control & transparency concerns

Managers want to know:

what is being said

when it’s being said

how it’s affecting deals

Objection phrasing:

“Can I see the conversations?”

“Can I control the messaging?”

🧠 BELIEFS (How they see the world)
1. Speed beats almost everything

Dealers strongly believe:

speed > polish

first contact wins

slow follow-up kills deals

Belief language:

“Fast beats perfect.”

“The first response gets the customer.”

2. Most sales work is repetitive and scriptable

Despite pride in sales skill, many admit:

80% of early conversations are the same

humans add value later, not at first touch

Belief phrasing:

“Same questions, same objections.”

“We repeat the same thing all day.”

3. Top performers are hard to clone

Managers believe:

their best salesperson has instincts

but those instincts are locked in one person

They want:

“Whatever my top guy does — do that automatically.”
