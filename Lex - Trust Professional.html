№ 01 Identity

Stillness, not silence. Composed, not minimal.

Lex is the system for buyers whose customers must feel safe before they feel anything else. It does not whisper — that's Folio's job. It does not yell — that's Riot's job. Lex composes: every element earns its place by sitting on the same baseline as something else, and the page reads like an annual report set by someone who has set a thousand of them.

If your buyer has a compliance department and a seal, this is their system.

The accent budget is the inverse of Riot. Lex spends ~10% of the viewport on its trust hue — usually a deep ink-navy, forest, or oxblood — and less than 2% on its accent (gold, sage, brick, steel-blue). The trust hue carries the page; the accent confirms a single thing per viewport. Never two accents. Never a gradient.

Borrowed vocabulary

  • The annual-report cover: outsized serif title, ledger row across the bottom, hairline rule above and below.
  • The prospectus TOC: dotted leaders, page numbers in the right column, hairline rules between rows.
  • The journal masthead: italic editorial subhead, all-caps small-caps section labels, Vol./No. metadata.
  • The certificate: embossed seal, italic signature, paper that is not white.

№ 02 Type

A sober transitional serif for headlines. A neutral humanist sans for body. Italics for emphasis where editorial.

Variants

  • Statesman — Source Serif 4 display + Inter Tight body. The financial-legal default.
  • Clinical — Lora display + Inter Tight body. Slightly softer terminals; reads calmer.
  • Civic — EB Garamond display + Inter Tight body. Old-style figures throughout.

No weight above 600. No display italic that isn't editorial. Body text uses old-style figures inline (1234567890); ledger rows and tabular data use lining figures ($1,234,567). Never mix.

№ 04 Decoration

Five permitted motifs. Use sparingly; one per section is plenty.

  • Hairline rules at 1px in --rule, full-width 2px in --ink at section breaks. The 2px rule is always doubled with a 1px rule 6px below — the annual-report double-rule.
  • Small-caps section labels in JetBrains Mono, 10–11px, 0.18em tracking. Always uppercase. Always with a Vol./No. or §-numeral.
  • Embossed seals. Outline circle, italic monogram inside. Used once on the page — the masthead.
  • Italic editorial pops. One italic phrase per heading, in the trust hue. Never decorative.
  • Dotted leaders on TOC-style rows (specimen tables, fee schedules). Always 4-letter-spaced periods, never solid lines.

What's banned: stamp shadows, drop shadows above 2px, gradients (any), marquees, two-accent compositions, full-bleed color sections that aren't the CTA, all-caps display.

Tokens, copy & paste.

The full token surface for the renderer. The user-configurable axes are --trust + --accent (palette, 9 options) and [data-variant] (Statesman · Clinical · Civic). Body always Inter Tight; display family swaps with variant.

:root tokens

--trust:        oklch(0.27 0.06 250);     /* deep navy */
--trust-deep:   oklch(0.18 0.06 250);     /* hover / press */
--trust-soft:   oklch(0.92 0.025 250);
--trust-ink:    oklch(0.98 0.005 250);    /* text on trust */
--accent:       oklch(0.74 0.10 80);      /* gold — < 2% of viewport */
--accent-deep:  oklch(0.58 0.11 80);
--accent-ink:   oklch(0.18 0.04 80);

/* Surface — never pure white */

--paper-1:      oklch(0.965 0.008 80);
--ink:          oklch(0.18 0.015 250);
--ink-2:        oklch(0.36 0.012 250);
--rule:         oklch(0.78 0.010 250);     /* hairline */

/* Type */
