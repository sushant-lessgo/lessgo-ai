01 Identity

Surge is for agencies that sell outcomes. Where Hearth is warm and photo-led and Riot is loud and brutalist, Surge is the proof-led middle: cool, composed, data-forward. It looks like the inside of a growth dashboard, because that's what the work produces.

The whole system orbits one job — make the visitor believe you'll move their number. The hero shows a live growth chart. Services are framed by the metric they improve. Case studies lead with the result, not the logo. Proof is the agency's own vocabulary: ROAS, impressions, organic traffic, pipeline.

If a section doesn't show a number you moved, it doesn't convince.

Five commitments

  • Metrics are the hero. Big tabular figures with delta chips (+312%, 3.4×) lead every block — never adjectives, never "we're passionate".
  • Dashboard grammar. CSS/SVG growth charts, KPI cards and channel-mix bars are the signature — the agency's craft, made visible.
  • Case studies as the proof spine. Each one is client + the metric moved + a sparkline. The portfolio convinces, not a manifesto.
  • Confident & kinetic, not loud. Cool slate ink, one electric accent, an ↗ motif. Energy through rising charts — not stamps, marquees or shouting.
  • Held semantic color. A fixed green/red marks up/down on charts; the brand accent swaps freely without ever confusing "good" and "bad".

What it is — and isn't

✕ Never

  • Stock "team high-fiving at laptops" photos
  • "Full-service · passionate · results-driven" lorem
  • Vanity stats with no client attached
  • Brutalist stamps or marquees (that's Riot)
  • Gradient-soup cards with a left accent border

✓ Always

  • A real, legible chart or KPI in the hero
  • Every claim tied to a named client + number
  • Channels named as tags (SEO, X, LinkedIn)
  • One held green for "up", one red for "down"
  • Specific, human copy — real names, real figures

Copy the tokens

Drop this into :root, then set data-palette and data-variant on <html>. The palette knob swaps only the accent hue; the held green/red never move. Everything else is baked.

Root + accent

/* Surge — base tokens */
:root {
  --h: 265;            /* accent hue — the only thing palette swaps */
  --c: 0.18;           /* accent chroma, held */

  --bg:        oklch(0.984 0.004 265);
  --surface:   oklch(1 0 0);
  --tint:      oklch(0.955 0.022 var(--h));

  --accent:     oklch(0.605 var(--c) var(--h));
  --accent-deep:oklch(0.50 calc(var(--c) + 0.02) var(--h));
  --accent-soft:oklch(0.93 0.055 var(--h));
  --accent-on:  oklch(0.99 0.012 var(--h));

  /* held semantic — never swap with palette */
  --pos:        oklch(0.66 0.16 152);
  --neg:        oklch(0.60 0.19 27);

  --panel:      oklch(0.205 0.028 265);
  --ink:        oklch(0.22 0.022 265);
  --ink-2:      oklch(0.42 0.02 265);
  --line:       oklch(0.905 0.01 265);

  --font-display: "Archivo";
  --font-body:    "Hanken Grotesk";
  --font-mono:    "JetBrains Mono";
  --font-serif:   "Instrument Serif";  /* editorial only */

  --r-sm: 7px; --r-md: 11px; --r-lg: 16px; --r-xl: 22px;
}

Variants

/* bold        — oversized type, accent-blocked hero, punchy */
