01 — IdentityWho this is for & how it should feel.

Warm, unhurried, human. Designed to make a small studio read as considered, not thin.

Hearth is for people who sell work they made with their hands — boutique agencies, one-human design studios, coaches, florists, garden designers, interior photographers, yoga teachers. The page should feel like stepping into a well-lit room, not a landing page. Where Meridian decorates with structure, Hearth decorates with breath: generous margins, italic serif for feeling, one hand-drawn mark, real photography framed with soft asymmetric radii.

It's a letter, not a brochure. The type does the tone, the photography carries the proof, the accent shows up only where it earns its place.

02 — TypographyTypography carries the warmth.

Pairing

  • Fraunces — display, variable. Optical size 144 at hero, 96 at section titles. SOFT axis 50. Italic is a deliberate rhetorical move, not an afterthought.
  • DM Sans — body. Modern, warm-neutral humanist. Keeps paragraphs readable without competing with the serif.
  • No mono. This system is not about machines.

Accent

A single warm accent. The user picks hue; we hold chroma (0.15) and lightness (0.62) so the accent always harmonizes with cream. Default: terracotta oklch(0.62 0.15 40).

Accent usage rules

  • Allowed: primary button fill, hand-drawn underline swoop under one hero word, quotation marks, numeric "01" in features, active nav underline, footer em-word, pricing "most booked" badge, link hover.
  • Forbidden: body text color, card fills, section backgrounds (use cream variants), accent icons sprinkled across a grid.
  • Budget: ≤ 8% of the visual weight of any viewport.

06 — Decorative layerHand-drawn, used once.

  • The swoop. A hand-drawn SVG underline beneath exactly one italic word in the hero. Never reused as a divider or decoration elsewhere.
  • Eyebrow rules. Two 28px hairlines flanking every eyebrow in accent. Replaces plain uppercase labels.
  • Photo collage. Hero is a 3-photo arrangement with one large petal-radius anchor and two smaller asymmetric crops. Every other section uses rectangular r-lg photos.
  • Laurel sprig. A thin-stroke SVG botanical sits in the CTA, top-right, and nowhere else.
  • Warm gradient. The closing CTA uses a subtle peach radial glow — the only gradient in the system. Sections are solid cream variants.

When in doubt, leave it plain. A well-set italic word is more decoration than a dozen ornaments.

07 — ComponentsButton, form, card, badge, iconography.

Buttons

  • Primary — accent fill, cream text, 14px 22px, 14px radius, warm accent shadow. Hover: accent-deep, lift 1px.
  • Ghost — cream fill, 1px sand border. Hover: border ink-3, bg cream-1.
  • Quiet — text only. For secondary nav, footer.
  • Small — 10px 16px, 10px radius.

Form input

Filled cream, 1px sand border, 10px radius. Focus: border pulls to accent, no glow. Labels above, not floating.

Cards

  • Flat — cream-1 bg, 1px sand border, 20px radius. Default.
  • Inverted — ink bg, cream text. Used for exactly one pricing tier (the middle).
  • Photo card — image at r-lg, caption in Fraunces italic below.

Badges

Pill. Italic Fraunces 13px. Accent fill + cream ink for status. Sand bg + ink-2 for neutral.

Iconography

Lucide is too uniform here. Preferred: number glyphs (italic Fraunces "01", "02", etc.) at card scale; hand-drawn marks (the swoop, the laurel) where a figurative icon would be; sage checkmarks in pricing. No icon grids. No 48px Lucide illustrations.

09 — Copy voiceHow the words should sound.

Warm, unhurried, first-person plural. Specific over clever.

We say "we visit," not "our consultation process." Concrete nouns over adjectives: "roses" not "stunning florals." Short sentences, occasional fragments for cadence. Permission to be a little literary — one italicized word per headline is allowed, even encouraged. Never "elevated," "curated," "bespoke," "transformative." Never exclamation marks. Numbers are specific: "three-person studio," not "small team."

Cadence: a long sentence, a short one. Set the scene, then land. Ledes are italic. The word "you" is earned.
