№ 01 Identity

A center of gravity none of the other eight systems will reach: clinical warmth — competent, calm, plainly bookable.

Vital exists because medicine has its own physics. Lex is too cold (you don't want a fiduciary energy from your dermatologist). Atlas is too cartographic (a visit isn't a journey). Hearth is too casual (you can't put "Hey friend" on an OB-GYN site). The buyer has to feel safe with their body or their kid's body before they'll feel anything else.

Browsable is a feature; bookable is a verb. Vital is built around the verb.

The visual grammar borrows from health-record interfaces — generous whitespace, calm sage, plain-language labels, real availability mockups — without inheriting the ugliness. Single warm clay accent for CTAs only. Never decoration.

Five commitments

  • Bookable, not browsable. Primary CTA everywhere is "Book a visit," with a real calendar mockup hero-side.
  • Conditions, not features. List what you actually treat, plain-language.
  • A face per clinician. Real bios, real credentials, a one-line "what they're like to work with."
  • Insurance + price clarity. A real "what it costs" strip — not buried in a footer link.
  • One calm color, one warm accent. Sage for surface; clay for CTA only. No decorative use of either.

№ 02 Type

Newsreader (variable serif) for display in Practice and Specialty; Inter Tight steps in for display in Modern Clinic. Inter Tight body throughout.

  • Practice — Newsreader 400 display, italic for emphasis. Reads warm and humane.
  • Modern Clinic — Inter Tight 600 display, Newsreader italic for emphasized phrase only. Reads app-like and decisive.
  • Specialty — Newsreader 400, larger, tighter tracking. Reads editorial.

№ 04 Decoration

Five permitted motifs. All borrowed from medical interfaces, intentionally calm.

  • Cross / plus. A single small + glyph for brand mark and item bullets. Never decorative on cards.
  • Sage halo. Soft radial gradient behind hero. The only allowed gradient in Vital.
  • Calendar booking card. Real-feeling availability mockup with date pill and three time slots — system signature.
  • Conditions grid. Plain-language list of what's treated, four columns, generous whitespace, hover arrow.
  • Italic lead, roman body. Editorial italic for one phrase per heading; never for paragraphs.

What's banned: stock photos of doctor-with-stethoscope, "modern healthcare experience" copy, gradient buttons, pulse-line ECG dividers as decoration, "AI-powered care," tablet-on-desk imagery, Lex's award-bar pattern, two-accent palettes (Riot territory).
